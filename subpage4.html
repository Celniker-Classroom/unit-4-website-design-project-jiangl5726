Other instruments similar to guitar!

There are so many stringed instruments that are similar to guitars like the Lute, Mandolin, ukulele, banjo, and so much more! Down below I am going to talk about the ones I listed.

Lute is different from guitars because it has a rounded back and shaped like a pear. The lute also usually have 15 strings and a different tuning system. Guitars usually has a flat back and fewer strings. One similarity though is the strings could be made out of nylon, and of course, they are both stringed instruments.

Madolin is also another stringed instrument that is both similar but different from guitar. It is generally smaller, with a rounded or teardrop looking body. It also usually has 8 strings that is arranged in 4 pairs. Madolin also has a more sharp and punchy sound compared to guitars. Some similarities include that they both have frets and are both used in genres like folk, rock, and classical.

One major difference is that ukuleles have 4 strings while guitars have 6. Ukuleles are also smaller, more lightweight, and super portable. It is also easier to play because of the fewer strings and simpler chords. Ukuleles have a Light, bright, cheerful, and “island-like” sound. Some similarities include they are both stringed instruments and could be strummed or fingerpicked. They also both have frets and similar note patterns.

Banjo is different from guitar as it has a drum-like body with a stretched membrane that gives it a sharp, twangy sound. Banjos also usually only have 5 strings compared to the guitar which has 6. Banjos also have a bright, metallic, punchy, and percussive sound. Similarities include they both have frets and you can play chords, melodies, and accompaniment with both. They are also both used in genres like folk, country, and Americana.
